# Product Copywriting — Deep Dive

## Benefit-Driven Writing

### The Feature → Benefit → Emotion Chain

Every product feature can be translated through three levels:
1. **Feature:** What it IS (factual, technical)
2. **Benefit:** What it DOES for the customer (practical)
3. **Emotion:** How it makes them FEEL (aspirational)

Example:
- Feature: "Made from recycled ocean plastic"
- Benefit: "Durable enough for daily use, won't crack or fade"
- Emotion: "Every purchase removes 5 bottles from the ocean — carry your values with you"

The best product copy hits all three levels, but leads with benefit.

## Sensory Writing Techniques

### Physical Products

Replace abstract descriptions with concrete sensory details:
- Instead of "high-quality leather," write "full-grain vegetable-tanned leather that develops a unique patina over time"
- Instead of "comfortable," write "the cushioned insole molds to your foot shape within the first week"

### Digital Products

For software, tools, and downloads, replace sensory language with experience language:
- Speed: "Exports a 10,000-row spreadsheet in under 2 seconds"
- Simplicity: "Set up in 3 clicks — no configuration files, no terminal commands"
- Reliability: "99.95% uptime over the last 12 months, with automated failover"

## Creating Urgency (Without Dishonesty)

Legitimate urgency:
- "Limited edition — only 200 units produced" (if true)
- "Sale ends Sunday at midnight" (if real deadline)
- "3 remaining at this price" (if accurate inventory)

Never fake urgency. Customers who feel manipulated don't return.

## Social Proof Integration

- **Review quotes:** "Best purchase I made this year" — Customer Name
- **Usage stats:** "Trusted by 10,000+ customers"
- **Expert endorsements:** "Recommended by [Authority]"
- **Media mentions:** "As featured in [Publication]"

Place social proof near the purchase decision point — after the description, before or alongside the price.

## Calls to Action

Product CTAs should be specific and action-oriented:
- "Add to Cart" (standard, clear)
- "Start Free Trial" (reduces commitment anxiety)
- "Get Yours" (creates ownership feeling)
- Avoid: "Submit," "Click Here," "Learn More" (vague, uninspiring)
