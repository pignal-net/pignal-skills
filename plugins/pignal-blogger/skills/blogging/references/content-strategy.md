# Content Strategy for Blogs

## Audience-First Content Planning

Content strategy starts with understanding who you're writing for and what they need — not what you want to say.

### Define Your Reader

Before planning content, answer:
- **Who are they?** Role, experience level, context
- **What problems do they face?** Day-to-day challenges
- **Where do they look for answers?** Search, social, communities
- **What would make them share your content?** Usefulness, novelty, validation

### Map Content to the Reader Journey

- **Awareness**: "I didn't know this was possible" — broad, introductory
- **Consideration**: "How does this compare to alternatives?" — comparative, analytical
- **Decision**: "How do I actually do this?" — step-by-step, practical

## Content Pillars and Themes

**Content pillars** are 3-5 core topics your blog consistently covers. They prevent topic drift and help readers know what to expect.

**Why pillars matter:** A blog about "whatever interests me" is a personal journal. A blog with clear pillars ("TypeScript patterns", "DevOps practices", "AI tooling") attracts a defined audience that grows over time.

**How to choose pillars:**
1. List your genuine areas of expertise
2. Cross-reference with what your audience needs
3. Pick 3-5 intersections where expertise meets need
4. Use Pignal's workspace system to organize content by pillar

## Frequency and Consistency

**Consistency beats frequency.** One quality post per week builds more audience than five mediocre posts followed by silence.

**Set a sustainable pace.** If you can write one excellent post per month, do that. If you can do weekly, great. The worst pattern is starting strong then going silent — it signals abandonment.

**Batch creation works.** Write 3-4 posts in one focused session, then schedule them. Use Pignal's `private` visibility to stockpile drafts, then vouch them on a regular cadence.

## Content Repurposing

One piece of content can serve multiple purposes:

- A detailed **Guide** can be broken into multiple **TILs**
- Several related **Insights** can be synthesized into a **Guide**
- A **Problem Solution** can become a talk, a thread, or a tutorial
- Long posts can produce standalone code snippets or checklists

**Tag strategically for repurposing.** Consistent tagging in Pignal makes it easy to find related content later when you want to synthesize or expand.

## Measuring Content Effectiveness

Without analytics, you're guessing. With Pignal's built-in page view tracking, look for:

- **Which posts get consistent traffic?** — These are your evergreen winners
- **Which tags get the most views?** — These are your strongest pillars
- **What's the referrer distribution?** — Search vs direct vs social tells you how people find you
- **Which posts have the highest time-on-page?** — Long engagement signals deep value

**Act on the data:**
- Double down on topics that consistently perform
- Update evergreen posts that still get traffic but have outdated information
- Archive or update posts that never found an audience
